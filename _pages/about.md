I am an environmental epidemiologist interested in how climate variability and environmental change shape infectious disease risk. I received my PhD in Environmental Health Sciences from the UC Berkeley School of Public Health, where I worked with Justin Remais, and I am currently a postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University School of Medicine working with Eran Bendavid. My work brings together epidemiology, climate science, spatial analysis, and machine learning to better understand when and where climate-sensitive diseases emerge, intensify, and shift over time.
